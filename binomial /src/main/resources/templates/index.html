Definition A binamial experiment is one that has these five characteristics:


  1. The experiment consists of n identical trials.
  2. Each trial results in one of two outcomes. For lack of a better name, the one out- come is called a success, S, and the other a failure, F.
  3. The probability of success on a single trial is equal to p and remains the same from trial to trial. The probability of failure is equal to (1-p) = q.
  4. The trials are independent.
  5. We are interested in x, the number of successes observed during the n trials, for x = 0, 1, 2, . . . , n.
